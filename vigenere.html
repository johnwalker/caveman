Vigenere Autokey Cipher

In an autokey cipher, the plaintext is incorporated into the key. Choose a password of length n to encrypt the first n characters of the plaintext. Those n characters of the plaintext are used to encrypt the following n characters. Characters that aren't in the alphabet are unchanged in the ciphertext, only characters that are in the alphabet may be in the password, and no password character is "skipped" because of plaintext that isn't in the alphabet. We repeat the process until all plaintext has been encrypted.
